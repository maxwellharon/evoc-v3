Discover Our Work

Executive Overview

EVOC serves as a strategic catalyst for development, focusing on imparting critical skills and knowledge necessary to prepare marginalized youth and women for formal and non-formal employment sectors.

By bridging the gap between a lack of formal education and entry into competitive labor markets, Empowering Voice of The Community (EVOC) acts as a partner of choice for sustainable poverty reduction and social equity.

Founded on the belief that sustainable development begins within communities, EVOC works alongside local residents to address the root causes of poverty, inequality, unemployment, and social exclusion through community-led solutions.

Our approach combines education, economic empowerment, health promotion, environmental sustainability, child protection, youth leadership, and community advocacy to create lasting social and economic change. By partnering with local leaders, schools, government institutions, private sector organizations, and development partners, EVOC strengthens community resilience while empowering individuals to become active agents of change within their own neighborhoods.

The EVOC Journey

From community consultations to lasting transformation.

Context & Institutional Origins

EVOC was established in 2018 following rigorous, localized field assessments within Nairobi's informal settlements, initially launching interventions within Mathare and Kibera — two of Nairobi's largest informal settlements where many households face interconnected challenges including unemployment, inadequate housing, limited access to healthcare and education, poor sanitation, and economic vulnerability.

The organization's founders identified a critical service gap regarding targeted socio-economic support structures for marginalized youth and women. Our interventions are designed in partnership with community members to address locally identified priorities and strengthen long-term resilience.

Methodology & Execution

Rather than deploying top-down solutions, EVOC explicitly develops programs built on strict problem identification, community prioritization, and demand-driven needs.

Today, the organization is anchored by a highly skilled, professional team explicitly focused on expanding community capacity, reducing vulnerability, and cultivating active participants in local economic ecosystems. We believe lasting change happens when communities lead their own development.

By partnering with local leaders, schools, government institutions, private sector organizations, and development partners, EVOC continues to build opportunities that enable vulnerable households to move from dependency toward dignity, self-reliance, and sustainable livelihoods.

Core Programmatic Pillars

EVOC deploys a multi-sectoral approach to community development, structured into distinct, high-impact programs.

Basic Education & Adult Literacy

Focus: Engaging illiterate and semi-literate youth and women in structured literacy tracks. Equips beneficiaries with functional knowledge to upgrade standard of living benchmarks and build parity with wider society.

We support children and young people through educational assistance, mentorship, life skills development, digital literacy, reading programs, and safe learning environments that improve school attendance, retention, and academic success.

📌 86 learners graduated; increased school retention

TVET & Entrepreneurship (TIVET)

Focus: Providing market-driven technical skills and foundational entrepreneurship toolkits. Directly bridges the gap between low formal educational attainment and successful labor market entry.

We equip young people with employability skills, entrepreneurship training, leadership development, vocational education, digital skills, and mentorship to improve access to meaningful employment and self-employment opportunities.

📌 210 trainees completed; over 60% employed or self-employed

Sexual Reproductive Health (SRH)

Focus: Delivering targeted awareness campaigns, behavioral modification training, and public health education. Minimizes high-risk behavior patterns, scales down transmission networks, and mitigates morbidity/mortality rates linked to HIV/AIDS.

We promote access to quality healthcare, mental health awareness, sexual and reproductive health education, nutrition, hygiene, and disease prevention to improve community wellbeing.

📌 500+ youth reached; increased health literacy

Sports & Culture

Focus: Nurturing raw creative and athletic talents while intentionally addressing underlying socio-behavioral trends. Serves as an effective, preventative diversion program to drastically lower crime and combat drug and substance abuse.

Harnessing sports and creative arts for youth empowerment, social cohesion, leadership development, and violence prevention.

📌 12 community teams formed; reduced youth crime

Environmental Conservation & Climate Action

Focus: Strengthening community resilience through waste management, urban greening, tree planting, recycling, and environmental education. We work with communities to improve waste management, promote recycling, urban greening, environmental education, clean-up campaigns, and climate resilience initiatives that create healthier neighborhoods.

📌 3,500 trees planted; cleaner neighborhoods

Community Development & Social Protection

Focus: Strengthening community systems, child protection, peacebuilding, advocacy, and social support for vulnerable households. We strengthen local governance, child protection systems, community participation, peacebuilding, and advocacy while supporting vulnerable households through targeted social interventions.

📌 Strengthened local governance & protection systems

Women's Economic Empowerment

Focus: Increasing women's economic participation through savings groups, financial literacy, enterprise development, and leadership. EVOC strengthens women's economic resilience through savings groups, financial literacy, business development support, vocational skills, and income-generating initiatives that increase household incomes and financial independence.

📌 1,800 women supported; increased household incomes

Where We Work

EVOC primarily serves communities in Mathare and Kibera, two of Nairobi's largest informal settlements, where vulnerable households face interconnected challenges.

Mathare

Our interventions in Mathare focus on education, youth skills training, health awareness, and environmental clean-ups. We work with local schools, youth groups, and women's networks to address high unemployment, poor sanitation, and limited access to services.

Key projects: Literacy classes, TVET training, SRH workshops, sports leagues, and tree planting.

Kibera

In Kibera, EVOC implements comprehensive community development programmes targeting women, youth, and children. Our work includes adult literacy, entrepreneurship, health outreach, child protection, and community governance.

Key projects: Women's savings groups, vocational training, mental health support, and community advocacy.

Theory of Change

How EVOC creates lasting change in communities.

Inputs

Community needs assessments, trained staff, partnerships, funding, local leadership engagement, and evidence-based programme design.

Activities

Literacy classes, vocational training, health workshops, sports leagues, tree planting, advocacy, savings groups, and mentorship.

Outputs

Number of learners trained, businesses started, trees planted, health sessions held, community groups formed, and policies influenced.

Outcomes

Improved literacy, increased employability, better health practices, stronger social cohesion, enhanced environmental stewardship, and women's empowerment.

Impact

Resilient communities with reduced poverty, gender equality, improved wellbeing, sustainable livelihoods, and active citizen participation.

Assumptions

Community ownership, inclusive participation, local capacity, supportive policy environment, and sustained partnerships.

Monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability & Learning (MEAL)

We are committed to evidence-based programming, transparency, and continuous improvement.

Monitoring

Regular data collection and tracking of programme activities, outputs, and outcomes using digital tools and community feedback.

Evaluation

Conducting baseline, mid-term, and end-line evaluations to measure impact, effectiveness, and sustainability of interventions.

Accountability

Ensuring transparency and responsiveness to communities, donors, and partners through regular reporting and feedback mechanisms.

Learning

Using data and lessons learned to adapt programmes, improve quality, and share best practices with stakeholders.
