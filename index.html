Who We Are

Who is EVOC?

Empowering Voice of The Community (EVOC) is a registered Community-Based Organization serving as a strategic catalyst for development in Nairobi's informal settlements.

Founded in 2018 following rigorous localized field assessments in Mathare and Kibera, EVOC works alongside community members to address the root causes of poverty, inequality, unemployment, and social exclusion through community-led solutions.

Our integrated development model combines education, technical and vocational skills, economic empowerment, health promotion, environmental stewardship, child protection, youth leadership, and community engagement to create sustainable pathways out of poverty.

Today, EVOC continues to build opportunities that enable vulnerable households to move from dependency toward dignity, self-reliance, and sustainable livelihoods.

Our Vision & Mission

The guiding principles that drive every initiative at EVOC.

🌟 Our Vision

Thriving, inclusive, and resilient communities where every person has the opportunity to realize their full potential.

We envision communities that are:

  • Safe – Free from violence, abuse, and exploitation
  • Healthy – With access to quality healthcare and wellbeing
  • Educated – Where every child and adult can learn and grow
  • Economically Empowered – With sustainable livelihoods for all
  • Environmentally Sustainable – Living in harmony with nature
  • Socially Inclusive – Where no one is left behind

🎯 Our Mission

To empower vulnerable communities through innovative, inclusive, and sustainable programs that promote:

  • Education – Expanding access to quality learning for all ages
  • Economic Empowerment – Building skills for meaningful employment
  • Health & Wellbeing – Improving physical and mental health outcomes
  • Environmental Sustainability – Protecting our planet for future generations
  • Youth & Women Leadership – Amplifying voices that matter
  • Community Participation – Ensuring local ownership of development

Together, we create opportunities that lead to lasting social and economic transformation.

What We Do

Our Focus Areas

Integrated, evidence-based interventions addressing the interconnected challenges facing vulnerable communities.

📚

Education & Skills Development

Expanding access to quality education and lifelong learning through literacy classes, mentorship, academic support, digital literacy, and safe learning environments that improve school attendance, retention, and academic success.

86 literacy graduates
💼

TVET & Entrepreneurship

Providing market-driven technical skills and foundational entrepreneurship toolkits. We equip young people with employability skills, vocational education, and mentorship to bridge the gap to meaningful employment and self-employment.

210 trainees completed; 60%+ employed
❤️

Health, SRH & Wellbeing

Delivering targeted awareness campaigns, behavioral modification training, and public health education. We promote mental health, nutrition, hygiene, and disease prevention to improve community wellbeing and reduce HIV prevalence.

500+ youth reached
🌿

Climate Action & Environmental Conservation

Strengthening community resilience through waste management, urban greening, tree planting, recycling, and environmental education. We work with communities to create cleaner, greener, and healthier neighborhoods.

3,500 trees planted
👩🏾‍🤝‍👩🏿

Women & Youth Empowerment

Strengthening economic resilience through savings groups, financial literacy, business development support, and income-generating initiatives. We amplify women's and youth voices in leadership and decision-making.

1,800 women supported
⚽

Sports, Arts & Culture

Nurturing creative and athletic talents while addressing underlying socio-behavioral trends. Sports and arts serve as effective diversion programs to reduce crime, combat substance abuse, and foster social cohesion.

12 community teams formed
Where We Work

Serving Nairobi's Informal Settlements

EVOC primarily serves communities in Mathare and Kibera, two of Nairobi's largest informal settlements where vulnerable households face interconnected challenges.

📍 Mathare

Our interventions in Mathare focus on education, youth skills training, health awareness, and environmental clean-ups. We work with local schools, youth groups, and women's networks to address high unemployment, poor sanitation, and limited access to services.

Key projects: Literacy classes, TVET training, SRH workshops, sports leagues, and tree planting.

📍 Kibera

In Kibera, EVOC implements comprehensive community development programmes targeting women, youth, and children. Our work includes adult literacy, entrepreneurship, health outreach, child protection, and community governance.

Key projects: Women's savings groups, vocational training, mental health support, and community advocacy.

Accountability

Monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability & Learning (MEAL)

We are committed to evidence-based programming, transparency, and continuous improvement.

Monitoring

Regular data collection and tracking of programme activities, outputs, and outcomes using digital tools and community feedback mechanisms.

Evaluation

Conducting baseline, mid-term, and end-line evaluations to measure impact, effectiveness, and sustainability of all interventions.

Accountability

Ensuring transparency and responsiveness to communities, donors, and partners through regular reporting and open feedback channels.

Learning

Using data and lessons learned to adapt programmes, improve quality, and share best practices with all stakeholders.

Safeguarding

Our Commitment to Safety & Dignity

At EVOC, safeguarding is not optional—it is a shared commitment and a core organizational value.

🛡️ EVOC Zero Tolerance Statement on Safeguarding

At Empowering Vulnerable and Orphaned Communities (EVOC), we are committed to creating and maintaining a safe, respectful, and inclusive environment for everyone we work with and serve. This includes children, young people, vulnerable adults, community members, staff, volunteers, consultants, interns, board members, partners, contractors, and visitors.

We recognize that every individual has the right to live free from abuse, exploitation, neglect, harassment, discrimination, and violence. Safeguarding is a fundamental responsibility that underpins all of our programmes, partnerships, and operations.

🚫 Zero Tolerance

EVOC maintains a zero-tolerance policy for all forms of: Sexual Exploitation, Sexual Abuse, Sexual Harassment, Child Abuse and Child Exploitation, Abuse or exploitation of vulnerable adults, Physical, emotional, verbal, or psychological abuse, Bullying and intimidation, Discrimination, Human trafficking, forced labour, and modern slavery, and Retaliation against individuals who report safeguarding concerns.

📢 Reporting Concerns: EVOC encourages all staff, volunteers, partners, beneficiaries, and members of the public to report any safeguarding concern without fear of intimidation or reprisal. All reports are handled with sensitivity, respect for confidentiality, and a survivor-centred approach.

🛡️ Protection from Retaliation: EVOC strictly prohibits retaliation against any person who, in good faith, reports a safeguarding concern or participates in an investigation.

🤝 Shared Responsibility: Safeguarding is everyone's responsibility. All EVOC employees, volunteers, consultants, board members, contractors, and partners are expected to uphold the highest standards of professional and ethical conduct.
